Discography


1989 (2014)
This is Taylor's latest album as well as the most successful one.
The album had already outsold the accumulated sales of both of her previous two albums, Speak Now and Red in the U.S.
In contrast to her previous work, the production of 1989 consists of drum programming, synthesizers, pulsating bass, processed backing vocals, and guitars. Furthermore, while the lyrical content is concerned with the familiar topics of love and relationships, Taylor's writing is less "boycentric," and frequently employs tropes showing greater sophistication.

Red (2012)
This is Taylor's 4th album.
The album title was inspired by the "semi-toxic relationships" that Taylor experienced during the process of conceiving this album, which Taylor described the emotions she felt as "red emotions" due to their intense and tumultuous nature. Red touches on her signature themes of love and heartbreak, however, from a more mature perspective while exploring other themes such as fame and the pressure of being in the limelight.

Speak Now (2010)
This is Taylor's 3rd album.
Production for the album took place during 2009 to 2010 at several recording studios, and was handled by Taylor Swift and Nathan Chapman. Written entirely by Taylor as the follow-up to Fearless (2008), Speak Now expands on the country pop style of her previous work, and features lyrical themes concerning love, romance and heartbreak.


Fearless (2008)
This is Taylor's 2nd album.
In this album, Taylor sought to prolong with centering romantic love the theme of the album. Primarily, she did not want to alienate her fans with songs about life on the road, for she could never relate to songs of the sort when younger.
She said, "I really try to write more about what I feel and guys and love because that's what fascinates me more than anything else - love and what it does to us and how we treat people and how they treat us. So pretty much every song on the album has a face that I associate with it."

Taylor Swift (2006)
This is Taylor's first album.
Taylor wrote songs for the album during her freshman year of high school, and has writing credits on all of the albums' songs including some of the co-written ones. She experimented with several album producers, ultimately choosing the producer of her demo album, Nathan Chapman. Musically, the album is country music styled and lyrically, it speaks of romantic relationships, a couple of which she wrote from observing relationships before being in one and also touches on her personal struggles in high school.
